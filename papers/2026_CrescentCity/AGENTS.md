## Agent Roles

### Research Agent
**Focus**: Close reading of the nested-systems history of Crescent City and Del Norte County

**Tasks**:
- Track the manuscript's four-part frame — Space, Time, People, Ideas/Minds — and keep findings tied to the part they come from.
- Separate paleoseismic *model output* (the ~37% / fifty-year M ≥ 8.0 estimate, the 10–15% full-margin M ≥ 9) from deterministic forecasting; preserve the author's hedged framing.
- Preserve the rapid AI-augmented synthesis context and the cited primary sources when summarizing claims.

### Methods Agent
**Focus**: Source provenance and scale alignment

**Tasks**:
- Document how geological, ecological, economic, political, and institutional scales are nested into one explanatory system via Ostrom social-ecological systems and Holling/Gunderson panarchy.
- Distinguish primary archival/agency sources (Census, paleoseismic turbidite studies, court records) from interpretive synthesis.
- Cross-check tsunami chronology (1946 / 1964 / 2011) and the 1964 warning-policy watershed against the cited literature.

### Citation Agent
**Focus**: DOI metadata and version tracking

**Tasks**:
- Use the Zenodo DOI `10.5281/zenodo.20286171` (version 1.0.0) as the canonical citation.
- Track future Zenodo versions or repository updates from `docxology/crescent_city`.
- Keep README, CITATION.cff, metadata.json, `paper_metadata.json`, `BIBLIOGRAPHY.md`, and `publications.html` JSON-LD synchronized.

### Synthesis Agent
**Focus**: Cross-domain relationships

**Tasks**:
- Connect this regional case study to the corpus's institutional / information-ecosystem and cognitive-security work without overclaiming a formal model.
- Surface the Del Norte education and Pelican Bay context as a bridge to under-served-community and civic-infrastructure themes.
- Treat the town as a worked example of coupled human–natural systems — the recurring hazard → rebuilding → governance → memory → adaptation loop — rather than a single-domain history.
