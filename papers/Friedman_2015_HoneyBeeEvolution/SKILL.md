# Large Scale Coding Sequence Change Underlies the Evolution of Post-developmental Novelty in Honey Bees

**Daniel A. Friedman, Brian R. Johnson** (2015) · Genetics & Biomedical Research

## Instructions

Use this skill when working with topics related to **honey bees, Apis mellifera, RNA-Seq, taxonomically restricted genes**.

When applying this skill:
1. Understand the evolution of novel traits through taxonomically restricted genes
1. Apply transcriptomic methods (RNA-Seq) to study tissue-specific gene expression in social insects
1. Analyze the molecular basis of postdevelopmental novelty in Apis mellifera

## Methods & Techniques

- RNA-Seq transcriptomics
- Differential gene expression analysis
- Taxonomically restricted gene (TRG) identification
- Coding sequence evolutionary rate analysis
- BLASTp homology searches
- Statistical enrichment tests

## Key Findings

- Demonstrates TRGs are enriched in novel honey bee tissues
- Provides RNA-Seq data for honey bee royal jelly, venom, and wax glands
- Supports the hypothesis that novel traits preferentially recruit novel genes
- Shows elevated rates of coding sequence change in TRG-enriched tissues
