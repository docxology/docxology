## Principal Collaborators

### Karl Friston — UCL, London

**Domains**: Active Inference, Free Energy Principle, Consciousness

Karl Friston is the originator of the free energy principle and Active Inference framework. He serves on the [AII Scientific Advisory Board](https://www.activeinference.institute/scientific-advisory-board) (2025–). Friedman and Friston's collaboration began with "Of woodlice and men" (2018, *ALIUS Bulletin*, 88 citations) and has continued through co-authored papers on variational synthesis, evolutionary dynamics (2023), federated inference (2024, 45 citations), and multi-agent Active Inference.

**Joint publications**: [WoodliceAndMen](../papers/2018_WoodliceAndMen/) · [VariationalSynthesis](../papers/2023_VariationalSynthesis/) · [FederatedInference](../papers/2024_FederatedInference/)

---

### Deborah Gordon — Stanford University

**Domains**: Entomology, Collective Behavior, Ecology

Deborah Gordon was Friedman's PhD advisor at Stanford (2014–2019). Their collaboration produced foundational work on the behavioral, physiological, and transcriptomic bases of collective behavior in harvester ants (*Pogonomyrmex barbatus*), with publications in *Cell*, *iScience*, *Scientific Reports*, and the *Annual Review of Neuroscience*.

**Joint publications**: [PhDDissertation](../papers/2019_PhDDissertation/) · [DopamineForaging](../papers/2018_DopamineForaging/) · [AntGenetics](../papers/2016_AntGenetics/) · [ForagingGene](../papers/2016_ForagingGene/) · [ForagerHydration](../papers/2019_ForagerHydration/)

---

### Brian Johnson — UC Davis

**Domains**: Social Insect Genomics, Distributed Physiology

Brian Johnson co-mentored Friedman's NSF Postdoctoral Fellowship at UC Davis (2020–2023), focusing on distributed physiology and evolutionary genomics in eusocial insects.

**Joint publications**: [DistributedPhysiology](../papers/2020_DistributedPhysiology/) · [GeneExpressionForagers](../papers/2020_GeneExpressionForagers/) · [NeurotransmitterVariation](../papers/2020_NeurotransmitterVariation/) · [HoneyBeeGeneExpression](../papers/2023_HoneyBeeGeneExpression/)

---

### Tim Linksvayer — Arizona State University

**Domains**: Evolutionary Genomics, Social Evolution

Tim Linksvayer co-mentored Friedman's postdoctoral work on evolutionary genomics in social insects, contributing to publications on gene expression, social complexity, and distributed physiology.

**Joint publications**: [BlattodeaDiversity](../papers/2024_BlattodeaDiversity/) · [DistributedPhysiology](../papers/2020_DistributedPhysiology/)

---

### Mahault Albarracin

**Domains**: Multi-Agent Active Inference, Shared Cognition

Albarracin serves on the [AII Scientific Advisory Board](https://www.activeinference.institute/scientific-advisory-board) (2025–). She and Friedman have collaborated on multi-agent Active Inference models, particularly on shared protentions (anticipatory representations) in multi-agent systems.

**Joint publications**: [SharedProtentions](../papers/2024_SharedProtentions/)

---

### Maxwell Ramstead

**Domains**: Active Inference, Multi-Scale Modeling, Phenomenology

Ramstead serves on the [AII Scientific Advisory Board](https://www.activeinference.institute/scientific-advisory-board) (2025–). He and Friedman have collaborated on formal Active Inference architectures and multi-agent systems, including work on shared protentions and federated inference.

**Joint publications**: [SharedProtentions](../papers/2024_SharedProtentions/) · [FederatedInference](../papers/2024_FederatedInference/)

---

### Eric Saund

**Domains**: Computational Modeling, Ant Foraging

Saund and Friedman developed a parsimonious single-pheromone model that accounts for empirical patterns of ant colony foraging previously explained by two-pheromone models.

**Joint publications**: [SinglePheromone](../papers/2023_SinglePheromone/)

---

### Chris Fields

**Domains**: Physics, Information Processing, Boundaries

Fields serves on the [AII Scientific Advisory Board](https://www.activeinference.institute/scientific-advisory-board) (2025–). He and Friedman have collaborated on foundational questions connecting physics, information processing, and Active Inference. Friedman produced the transcript and educational materials for Fields' "Physics as Information Processing" course at the Active Inference Institute.

---

### Riddhi Pitliya

**Domains**: Thoughtseeds Framework, Meditative States, Active Inference

Pitliya and Friedman developed the Thoughtseeds framework — a hierarchical and agentic model for investigating thought dynamics in meditative states using Active Inference.

**Joint publications**: [Thoughtseeds](../papers/2025_Thoughtseeds/) · [SharedProtentions](../papers/2024_SharedProtentions/)

---

### Axel Constant

**Domains**: Variational Methods, Evolutionary Dynamics, Developmental Biology

Constant contributed to the variational synthesis of evolutionary and developmental dynamics with Friedman and Friston.

**Joint publications**: [VariationalSynthesis](../papers/2023_VariationalSynthesis/)

---

### Thomas Parr — UCL, London

**Domains**: Active Inference, Variational Methods, Computational Neuroscience

Thomas Parr is a leading researcher in Active Inference and computational neuroscience at UCL, and co-author of the *Active Inference* textbook (Parr, Pezzulo & Friston, 2022). Parr and Friedman have collaborated on variational synthesis and multi-agent Active Inference architectures.

**Joint publications**: [VariationalSynthesis](../papers/2023_VariationalSynthesis/) · [FederatedInference](../papers/2024_FederatedInference/)

---

### Eirik Søvik

**Domains**: Consciousness Studies, Social Insects

Søvik and Friedman explored whether ant colonies can serve as test cases for scientific theories of consciousness.

**Joint publications**: [AntConsciousness](../papers/2019_AntConsciousness/)

---

### Robert (RJ) Cordes

**Domains**: Digital Rhetoric, Cognitive Security, Meme Research, Information Science

Cordes is the founder of COGSEC.org (Cognitive Security and Education Forum, 2018), with Friedman as honorary co-founder (2019). Together they developed cognitive security frameworks including ATLAS (question-oriented pattern languages for knowledge management), COGSEC volumes IRT-20 (2020), NIM-21 (2021), CAT-22 (2022), and the Knowledge Management Archipelago. Cordes and Friedman also developed multidisciplinary approaches to studying image memes and digital rhetoric at scale.

**Joint publications**: [Facilitator's Catechism](../papers/2020_FacilitatorsCatechism/) · [ImageMemeResearch](../papers/2024_ImageMemeResearch/) · [DigitalRhetorical](../papers/2021_DigitalRhetorical/) · [P3IF](../papers/2023_P3IF/) · [CognitiveIntegrity](../papers/2026_CognitiveIntegrity/)

---

### Conor Heins — VERSES AI Research Lab

**Domains**: Active Inference, Computational Modeling, AI Systems

Heins is a researcher at the VERSES AI Research Lab working on computational implementations of Active Inference. He and Friedman have collaborated on recent Active Inference papers involving scalable implementations and belief-based architectures.

**Joint publications**: Recent co-authored Active Inference papers (2024)

---

### Tim Verbelen — VERSES AI Research Lab

**Domains**: Active Inference, Machine Learning, Distributed Systems

Verbelen is a researcher at the VERSES AI Research Lab focusing on Active Inference implementations and distributed intelligence. He has co-authored papers with Friedman on federated inference and scalable Active Inference architectures.

**Joint publications**: Recent co-authored Active Inference papers (2024)

---

### Mariana Mascarenhas

**Domains**: Information Science, Digital Media

Mascarenhas contributed to the multidisciplinary paradigm for scaling up qualitative analyses of image memes.

**Joint publications**: [ImageMemeResearch](../papers/2024_ImageMemeResearch/)
