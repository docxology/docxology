'''Daniel Ari Friedman''' is an American researcher, educator, and artist whose work spans [[entomology]], [[active inference]], [[cognitive security]], [[synergetics (Fuller)|synergetics]], and [[computational biology]]. He is the co-founder and president of the [[Active Inference Institute]], a [[501(c)(3)]] non-profit educational organization dedicated to the study and application of [[active inference]].<ref name="aii-officers">{{cite web |url=https://www.activeinference.institute/officers |title=Officers |website=Active Inference Institute |access-date=2026-05-13}}</ref> The docxology [https://github.com/docxology/docxology/blob/main/pages/BIBLIOGRAPHY.md online bibliography] catalogs 154 works including papers, books, presentations, courses, and recent deposits.<ref name="bibliography">{{cite web |url=https://github.com/docxology/docxology/blob/main/pages/BIBLIOGRAPHY.md |title=Complete Bibliography – Daniel Ari Friedman |website=GitHub |access-date=2026-06-04}}</ref>

== Education ==

Friedman received his [[Bachelor of Science|BS]] in [[Genetics]] from the [[University of California, Davis]] in 2014.<ref name="orcid">{{cite web |url=https://orcid.org/0000-0001-6232-9096 |title=Daniel Ari Friedman – ORCID |access-date=2026-03-24}}</ref>

He earned his [[Doctor of Philosophy|PhD]] in Ecology and Evolution from [[Stanford University]] in 2019, under the supervision of [[Deborah M. Gordon]].<ref name="dissertation">{{cite thesis |type=PhD |last=Friedman |first=Daniel Ari |title=Behavioral, Physiological, and Transcriptomic Variation Among Colonies of the Red Harvester Ant (Pogonomyrmex barbatus) |publisher=Stanford University |year=2019 |url=http://purl.stanford.edu/pb813wm1484}}</ref> His doctoral research was conducted at [[Jasper Ridge Biological Preserve]], where he studied the behavioral, physiological, and transcriptomic bases of collective behavior in red harvester ants (''[[Pogonomyrmex barbatus]]'').<ref name="jrbp">{{cite web |url=https://jrbp.stanford.edu/content/ant-survey-participants |title=Ant Survey Participants |website=Jasper Ridge Biological Preserve |access-date=2026-03-24}}</ref>

From 2020 to 2023, Friedman held an [[National Science Foundation|NSF]] Postdoctoral Research Fellowship in Biology at the [[University of California, Davis]], Department of Entomology and Nematology, co-mentored by Brian Johnson (UC Davis) and Tim Linksvayer ([[Arizona State University]]).<ref name="orcid" />

== Career ==

=== Active Inference Institute ===

Friedman co-founded the Active Inference Institute, which emerged from foundational team meetings in 2019–2020 (initially as the Active Inference Lab). The organization was registered as a non-profit in Delaware in mid-2022 and received official 501(c)(3) recognition in 2024, supported pro bono by Fried Frank law firm.<ref name="aii-history">{{cite web |url=https://www.activeinference.institute/history |title=History |website=Active Inference Institute |access-date=2026-03-25}}</ref> He serves as President and Treasurer. Current officers include Alexandra Mikhailova as Vice-President and Secretary (2025–).<ref name="aii-officers" /> The Board of Directors currently lists Alexander Sabine, Alianna J. Maren, Ana Magdalena Hurtado, Ann Stapleton, Austin Cook, Daniel Friedman, Edward Ober, Ellynne Dec, V. Bleu Knight, and Vladimir Baulin.<ref name="aii-board">{{cite web |url=https://www.activeinference.institute/board-of-directors |title=Board of Directors |website=Active Inference Institute |access-date=2026-05-13}}</ref> The Institute has produced over 500 educational videos, organized annual Applied Active Inference Symposia, and developed open-source research tools. Its Scientific Advisory Board has included researchers such as Karl Friston, Maxwell Ramstead, Mahault Albarracin, and Chris Fields, with a 2026 SAB cohort listed on the Institute site.<ref name="aii-sab">{{cite web |url=https://www.activeinference.institute/scientific-advisory-board |title=Scientific Advisory Board |website=Active Inference Institute |access-date=2026-05-13}}</ref>

=== Academic teaching ===

Friedman teaches at the [[College of the Redwoods]] as Associate Faculty in Biology, including **BIOL-1** (General Biology, Pelican Bay) and **BIOL-8** (Human Biology) in Spring 2026.<ref name="profile-repo">{{cite web |url=https://github.com/docxology/docxology/blob/main/pages/PROFILE.md |title=PROFILE.md – Daniel Ari Friedman |website=GitHub |access-date=2026-04-25}}</ref> He has also developed online courses on remote team communication through [[Udemy]].<ref name="udemy-remote">{{cite web |url=https://www.udemy.com/course/communication-for-remote-teams/ |title=Communication for Remote Teams |website=Udemy |access-date=2026-03-24}}</ref>

== Research ==

=== Entomology and collective behavior ===

Friedman's doctoral and postdoctoral research centers on harvester ants (''[[Pogonomyrmex barbatus]]''), investigating how individual-level neurobiology, gene expression, and neurochemistry give rise to colony-level collective behavior. A key finding from his work demonstrated that [[dopamine]] regulates collective foraging decisions in harvester ant colonies, published in ''[[iScience]]''.<ref name="dopamine">{{cite journal |last1=Friedman |first1=Daniel A. |last2=Pilko |first2=Anna |last3=Skowronska-Krawczyk |first3=Dorota |last4=Krasber |first4=Kelley |last5=Parker |first5=Julia |last6=Jay |first6=Sara |last7=Gordon |first7=Deborah M. |title=The Role of Dopamine in Collective Regulation of Foraging in Harvester Ants |journal=iScience |year=2018 |doi=10.1016/j.isci.2018.09.001}}</ref> His research has also shown that brain gene expression varies with colony-level behavioral traits, published in ''[[Communications Biology]]''.<ref name="gene-expression">{{cite journal |last1=Friedman |first1=Daniel A. |display-authors=etal |title=Gene expression variation in the brains of harvester ant foragers is associated with collective behavior |journal=Communications Biology |year=2020 |volume=3 |doi=10.1038/s42003-020-0813-8}}</ref>

With Eirik Søvik, Friedman published a philosophical analysis of whether ant colonies can serve as test cases for scientific theories of consciousness in ''[[Synthese]]''.<ref name="ant-consciousness">{{cite journal |last1=Friedman |first1=Daniel A. |last2=Søvik |first2=Eirik |title=The ant colony as a test for scientific theories of consciousness |journal=Synthese |year=2019 |doi=10.1007/s11229-019-02130-y}}</ref> His co-authored review on ant genetics appeared in the ''[[Annual Review of Neuroscience]]''.<ref name="ant-genetics">{{cite journal |last1=Friedman |first1=Daniel A. |last2=Gordon |first2=Deborah M. |title=Ant Genetics: Reproductive Physiology, Worker Morphology, and Behavior |journal=Annual Review of Neuroscience |year=2016 |volume=39 |doi=10.1146/annurev-neuro-070815-013927}}</ref>

=== Active inference ===

Friedman has co-authored work with [[Karl Friston]] and collaborators on applications of the [[free energy principle]] across biological and cognitive scales. Their first joint publication, "Of woodlice and men: A Bayesian account of cognition, life and consciousness" (2018), appeared in the ''ALIUS Bulletin''.<ref name="woodlice">{{cite journal |last1=Friedman |first1=Daniel A. |last2=Friston |first2=Karl J. |title=Of woodlice and men: A Bayesian account of cognition, life and consciousness |journal=ALIUS Bulletin |year=2018 |doi=10.34700/h460-nz89}}</ref> Subsequent collaborations with Friston, Thomas Parr, Axel Constant, and others produced "A variational synthesis of evolutionary and developmental dynamics" in ''[[Entropy (journal)|Entropy]]''<ref name="variational">{{cite journal |last1=Friston |first1=Karl J. |last2=Friedman |first2=Daniel A. |display-authors=etal |title=A Variational Synthesis of Evolutionary and Developmental Dynamics |journal=Entropy |year=2023 |volume=25 |issue=7 |page=964 |doi=10.3390/e25070964}}</ref> and "Federated inference and belief sharing" in ''[[Neuroscience & Biobehavioral Reviews]]''.<ref name="federated">{{cite journal |last1=Friedman |first1=Daniel A. |display-authors=etal |title=Federated inference and belief sharing |journal=Neuroscience & Biobehavioral Reviews |year=2024 |doi=10.1016/j.neubiorev.2023.105500}}</ref> More recent collaborations with Conor Heins and Tim Verbelen at the VERSES AI Research Lab have advanced computational implementations of active inference.<ref name="denise-holt">{{cite web |url=https://deniseholt.us/active-inference-pioneering-a-new-era-of-artificial-intelligence/ |title=Active Inference: Pioneering a New Era of Artificial Intelligence |author=Denise Holt |year=2024 |access-date=2026-03-25}}</ref>

Notable frameworks developed by Friedman include CEREBRUM (Case-Enabled Reasoning Engine with Bayesian Representations for Unified Modeling),<ref name="cerebrum">{{cite report |last=Friedman |first=Daniel A. |title=CEREBRUM: Case-Enabled Reasoning Engine with Bayesian Representations for Unified Modeling |year=2025 |doi=10.5281/zenodo.15170907}}</ref> Generalized Notation Notation (GNN) for specifying active inference models,<ref name="gnn">{{cite report |last=Friedman |first=Daniel A. |title=Generalized Notation Notation for Active Inference Models |year=2023 |doi=10.5281/zenodo.7803328}}</ref> and the "Thoughtseeds" framework for modeling thought dynamics in meditative states, published in ''[[Entropy (journal)|Entropy]]''.<ref name="thoughtseeds">{{cite journal |last1=Friedman |first1=Daniel A. |last2=Pitliya |first2=Riddhi J. |display-authors=etal |title=Thoughtseeds: A Hierarchical and Agentic Framework for Investigating Thought Dynamics in Meditative States |journal=Entropy |year=2025 |volume=27 |issue=5 |page=459 |doi=10.3390/e27050459}}</ref>

=== Cognitive security ===

Through COGSEC.org (the Cognitive Security and Education Forum, informally founded 2018 by RJ Cordes, with Friedman as honorary co-founder by 2019 — established in response to Rand Waltzman's 2017 [[United States Senate Committee on Armed Services|Senate Armed Services Committee]] testimony on "The Weaponization of Information"),<ref name="cogsec-about">{{cite web |url=https://www.cogsec.org/about-2 |title=About |website=COGSEC.org |access-date=2026-03-25}}</ref> Friedman has authored three books on narrative security and information ecosystems: ''The Great Preset: Remote Teams and Operational Art'' (IRT-20, 2020), ''Narrative Information Ecosystems: Conflict and Trust on the Endless Frontier'' (NIM-21, 2021), and ''Structuring the Information Commons: Open Standards and Cognitive Security'' (CAT-22, 2022).<ref name="bibliography" /> His core frameworks include P3IF (Properties, Processes, and Perspectives Inter-Framework),<ref name="p3if">{{cite report |last=Friedman |first=Daniel A. |title=P3IF: Properties, Processes, and Perspectives Inter-Framework |year=2023 |doi=10.5281/zenodo.7093837}}</ref> developed in affiliation with DHS Office of Health Security, UW Applied Physics Lab, Pivot for Humanity, and UC Davis; and the Cognitive Integrity Framework for formal multiagent security.<ref name="cog-integrity">{{cite report |last=Friedman |first=Daniel A. |title=Cognitive Integrity: Formal Foundations for Multiagent Security |year=2025 |doi=10.5281/zenodo.18364119}}</ref> His work applies active inference and [[complexity science]] to understand information ecosystems, trust systems, and AI accountability. He has submitted policy comments to the [[National Telecommunications and Information Administration|NTIA]] on AI accountability.<ref name="ntia">{{cite report |last=Friedman |first=Daniel A. |title=Comments on AI Accountability Policy to NTIA |year=2023 |doi=10.5281/zenodo.8025957}}</ref>

=== Published interviews ===

Friedman has conducted published interviews with notable researchers for the ''ALIUS Bulletin'', the publication of the ALIUS Research Group (an international collective investigating alterations of consciousness).<ref name="alius">{{cite web |url=https://www.aliusresearch.org/ |title=ALIUS Research Group |access-date=2026-03-24}}</ref> His interview subjects include [[Daniel Dennett]] (on philosophy of mind, 2019),<ref name="dennett">{{cite journal |last=Friedman |first=Daniel A. |title=Dennett Explained |journal=ALIUS Bulletin |year=2019 |doi=10.34700/7gkw-zh08}}</ref> [[Robert Sapolsky]] (on free will, 2022),<ref name="sapolsky">{{cite journal |last=Friedman |first=Daniel A. |title=On free will or the lack thereof |journal=ALIUS Bulletin |year=2022 |doi=10.5281/zenodo.7394901}}</ref> and Timothy Hanks (on decision neuroscience, 2020).<ref name="hanks">{{cite journal |last=Friedman |first=Daniel A. |title=The neuroscience of decision making |journal=ALIUS Bulletin |year=2020 |doi=10.34700/8pg4-0h12}}</ref>

== Art ==

=== Visual art practice ===

Friedman's visual art practice encompasses abstract black-and-white pen-and-ink drawings, often featuring geometric and tetrahedral motifs inspired by [[Buckminster Fuller]]'s [[synergetics (Fuller)|synergetics]].<ref name="curio-medium">{{cite web |url=https://medium.com/@curiocards/daniel-friedman-decentralized-beauty-nft-art-and-ants-7dc664b72534 |title=Daniel Friedman — Decentralized Beauty: NFT Art and Ants |website=Medium |publisher=Curio Cards |access-date=2026-03-24}}</ref> His collaborative art paradigm "Partner Pen Play in Parallel" (PPPiP) was published in the journal ''Arts''.<ref name="pppip">{{cite journal |last=Friedman |first=Daniel A. |title=Partner Pen Play in Parallel (PPPiP): A New Paradigm for Relationship Improvement |journal=Arts |year=2018 |volume=7 |issue=3 |page=39 |doi=10.3390/arts7030039}}</ref>

His scholarship on art and philosophy includes papers on [[William Blake]] and Buckminster Fuller, including comparative studies of the two figures<ref name="blake-fuller">{{cite report |last=Friedman |first=Daniel A. |title=William Blake & Buckminster Fuller: Lives in Juxtaposition |year=2023 |doi=10.5281/zenodo.7514368}}</ref> and Active Inference interpretations of Blake's theory of perception.<ref name="doors">{{cite report |last=Friedman |first=Daniel A. |title=The Doors of Perception are the Threshold of Prediction: Active Inference and William Blake's Theory of Seeing |year=2026 |doi=10.5281/zenodo.18600041}}</ref>

=== Curio Cards (NFT art) ===

On May 9, 2017, three of Friedman's pen drawings were included in the [[Curio Cards]] project, recognized in NFT archaeology as an early art [[non-fungible token|NFT]] collection on the [[Ethereum]] mainnet, predating [[CryptoPunks]] by approximately six weeks.<ref name="nft-archaeology">{{cite web |url=https://nftarchaeology.io/nft-details/curiocards |title=Curio Cards |website=NFT Archaeology |access-date=2026-03-25}}</ref><ref name="curio-docs">{{cite web |url=https://docs.curio.cards/the-artists/daniel-friedman |title=Daniel Friedman – Curio Cards Artist Documentation |website=docs.curio.cards |access-date=2026-03-25}}</ref> His Card 24 ("Complexity", 333 copies) depicts fragmented geometric fields inspired by ant colonies and complex systems; Card 25 (222 copies) explores rhythmic complexity patterns; Card 26 (106 copies, rarest in set) employs a playful decreasing supply mechanic.<ref name="curio-details">{{cite web |url=https://curio.cards/card/24 |title=Card 24 |website=Curio Cards |access-date=2026-03-25}}</ref> The project was founded by Thomas Hunt, Travis Uhrig, and Rhett Creighton, comprising 30 unique card series from seven artists (Cryptograffiti, Cryptopop, Daniel Friedman, Marisol Vengas, Phneep, Robek World, Thoros of Myr), originally sold on-chain for $0.50–$1.00 per card using individual ERC-20 contracts (predating ERC-721).<ref name="curio-medium" />

A full set of 30 Curio Cards plus the rare "17b" misprint was auctioned at [[Christie's]] on October 1, 2021, selling for over **$1.2 million (393 ETH)** — the first [[Ether (cryptocurrency)|ETH]]-denominated auction at Christie's.<ref name="christies">{{cite web |url=https://www.christies.com/en/lot/lot-6337619 |title=Curio Cards – Christie's Lot 6337619 |website=Christie's |date=2021-10-01 |access-date=2026-03-25}}</ref> Friedman contributed a chapter on Curio Cards to the [[Taschen]] book ''On NFT'' (2024, {{ISBN|978-3-8365-9970-2}}).<ref name="bibliography" />

== Organizations ==

=== Complexity Weekend ===

Since 2019, Friedman has been co-founder and co-organizer of Complexity Weekend (also known as Complexity Adventures), a series of weekend-long hackathons that form diverse global teams to address problems using [[complexity science]].<ref name="complexity-adventures">{{cite web |url=https://www.complexityadventures.com/organizers/2 |title=Daniel Ari Friedman – Organizer |website=Complexity Adventures |access-date=2026-03-24}}</ref>

=== COGSEC.org ===

Friedman is an honorary co-founder of COGSEC.org, an organization focused on cognitive security — the protection and strengthening of information ecosystems through formal frameworks, open standards, and applied research.<ref name="bibliography" />
